I've always liked writing by mixing a bit of storytelling with the main topic. For me, that makes the content more human and tangible, as if there's a conversation behind the text.

Curiously, I didn't always think this way. When I was younger and came across blogs — back when BlogSpot was still living its glory days — I used to turn my nose up at them. I found everything excessively long and, to be honest, quite tedious. I wanted to get straight to the point and didn't understand why anyone would spend time reading about someone else's experience.

As it turns out, this time I was completely wrong.

As I entered adulthood, I developed a taste for reading and writing that I never would have imagined having a few years earlier. I began to see value not just in the information itself, but also in the story that comes with it. After all, it's often the context that transforms a simple account into something worth remembering.

I found in writing a way to give voice to thoughts that previously remained trapped in my mind and, in a way, I believe that writing code also fits into that.

As a software developer, it's natural that many of the texts published here end up orbiting the world of technology. Programming, system architecture, tools, productivity, continuous learning and the challenges that arise throughout a career will certainly appear frequently. Still, I don't intend to limit this space to just the technical side. Some experiences, reflections and interests that cross my path may also find a place here.

In the end, inspired by people I admire and the stories I've followed over the years, I decided to create a space of my own to do the same: a blog where I can record technical learnings for future reference, share experiences and, every now and then, simply muse a little while organizing my thoughts on some subject that's occupying my mind.

Ultimately, I intend to write only about what I truly consider relevant. The idea is not to turn this space into an endless collection of texts and tutorials about specific tools, but rather to keep it as a record of learnings, experiences and reflections that, for some reason, I consider important enough to share or revisit in the future.

Perhaps the greatest use of this blog is to serve as a kind of external memory, a place to record ideas before they get lost with time. Still, if any of these stories, learnings or reflections ends up being useful to someone who stumbles upon this place, then it will have already fulfilled a greater purpose than the one that motivated its creation.
